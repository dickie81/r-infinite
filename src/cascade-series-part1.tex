\subsection{How many dimensions does the observer have?}

QFT's zero-point vacuum energy density sums the ground-state energies
of every mode of every free field on flat Minkowski space

\begin{equation}\label{eq:qft-vacuum}
\rho_\Lambda^{\mathrm{QFT}} \;=\; \sum_{\text{fields}}\int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\,\frac{\hbar\omega_k}{2}

The integration is over 3-momentum space at fixed time, with the
implicit assumption that time is a free fourth dimension: the
Hamiltonian $H$ generates unbounded unitary time evolution, and the

\begin{equation}\label{eq:cascade-vacuum}
\rho_\Lambda^{\mathrm{cascade}} \;\propto\; \sum_{d=d_1}^{d_2}\Omega_{d-1}

\[
\Omega_3 \;=\; \frac{\pi}{2}\,\Omega_2 \qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad \frac{\Omega_2}{\Omega_3} \;=\; \frac{2}{\pi}.

Per-$S^2$ content density (the natural cascade-frame object at each

\subsection{Reframing the cosmological constant problem}

The standard statement of the cosmological constant problem assumes QFT
is giving a genuine estimate of $\rho_\Lambda$ that happens to be
$10^{120}$ too large, so that some unknown physics must cancel it down
to the observed value. The cascade reframes this as three separable
structural disagreements, none of which requires a cancellation:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{The number of free dimensions is different.} QFT counts
modes over 4 free dimensions (flat $\mathbb{R}^{3,1}$, with time
unbounded). The cascade counts modes over 3 free dimensions (the
angular $S^3$), because time is locked as the observer's asymptotic
infall. The Morse foliation of $S^3$ by 2-spheres gives the
projection factor $\Omega_2/\Omega_3 = 2/\pi$, i.e., $O(1)$, not
$O(10^{120})$. This $\pi/2$ is a geometric cost of the 3D projection;
it has nothing to do with fine-tuning.
\item \emph{The spectrum of modes is different.} QFT integrates a
continuous mode density $d^3k/(2\pi)^3$ and diverges as
$k_{\max}^4$. The cascade sums a finite discrete tower of 198 layers
and converges by construction. This is the $\sim 10^{120}$ factor.
It is not a miracle cancellation in the cascade; it is the structural
fact that the cascade's tower is finite, while QFT's mode integral is
infinite before the cutoff is imposed.
\item \emph{The UV regulator is different.} QFT imposes $k_{\max}
\sim M_{\mathrm{Pl}}$ by hand. The cascade terminates at $d_2 = 217$
by theorem --- specifically, by Part~0 Theorem~6.7, which identifies
$d_2$ as the unique integer at which the recurrence's second
canonical constant $c_2 = \sqrt{\pi}$ is reached by the decay rate.
The terminus has no parameter-counting cost. QFT's cutoff does.
\end{enumerate}

This does not ``solve'' the cosmological constant problem in the sense
of showing how QFT's naive answer becomes the cascade's natural answer
through some field-theoretic mechanism. The cascade is not a refinement
of QFT's calculation. It is an alternative architecture for counting
vacuum content, grounded in three architectural commitments that QFT
does not make: time is locked as the observer's infall; the spectrum
of cascade layers is discrete and finite; the terminus is forced by
the Gamma function rather than imposed by hand. The $\pi/2$ projection
factor of Theorem~3.1 is the consequence of the first commitment
(locked time) and is the only such factor of order unity that the
cascade needs at the observer's frame; the remaining $\sim 10^{120}$
difference between QFT's naive estimate and the observed value is
accounted for by the second and third commitments together.
