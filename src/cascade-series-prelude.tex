\begin{abstract}
A theory of everything cannot have inputs: every input demands an
explanation of its origin, and that explanation is either circular or
requires a deeper theory. The only starting point that requires no
explanation is nothing. This prelude shows that ``nothing,'' taken
seriously as a mathematical object, is the infinite-dimensional unit
ball---and that the unit ball is not featureless. It has a slicing
recurrence, a unique constant ($\sqrt{\pi}$), four distinguished
dimensions, and a cascade invariant of $10^{-120}$. These are not
imposed; they are consequences of the single pre-mathematical fact that
false and true are distinguishable. The series that follows is the
derivation of what that fact implies about the geometry of the world.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Problem with Starting Points}\label{sec:starting-points}
% ============================================================

Every theory of everything proposed to date begins with something:
a string, a spin network, a set of fields, a symmetry group, a
Lagrangian, a wave function, a set of rules. Each starting point
carries structure, and that structure demands explanation.

Why a string rather than a membrane? Why $\mathrm{SU}(3)\times
\mathrm{SU}(2)\times\mathrm{U}(1)$ rather than $\mathrm{SU}(5)$?
Why ten dimensions rather than eleven? Every choice is a free input.
Every free input is a knob that could have been set differently. Every
knob that could have been set differently invites the question: what
set it?

The options are limited. Either the starting point is explained by
something deeper---in which case it was not the starting point---or it
is accepted as given, and the theory is incomplete. A theory of
everything that begins with something is, by construction, not a
theory of everything. It is a theory of everything \emph{given} its
starting point.

The alternative is to begin with nothing.

\section{What ``Nothing'' Means}\label{sec:what-nothing-means}
% ============================================================

``Nothing'' in physics is usually taken to mean empty space, or the
quantum vacuum, or the absence of matter. These are not nothing. Empty
space has dimension, signature, topology. The quantum vacuum has
energy, fluctuations, fields. The absence of matter presupposes a
stage on which matter is absent.

Mathematical nothing is more austere: no space, no time, no
dimension, no content. But pure nothing---the absence of all
structure---cannot even be discussed, because discussion requires
the distinction between a statement and its negation.

This is the key observation. The minimal structure required for any
formal system to exist is not a space, not a symmetry, not a
dynamical law. It is the distinction between true and false.

\begin{definition}[Pre-mathematical axiom]\label{def:axiom}
$0 \neq 1$.
\end{definition}

This is not an axiom in the usual sense. It is not chosen from a
menu of alternatives. It is the precondition for axioms, theorems,
and proofs to be meaningful. A system that cannot distinguish $0$
from $1$ cannot state any proposition, cannot derive any consequence,
cannot describe any world. Definition~\ref{def:axiom} is the cost of
admission for coherent thought.

\begin{principle}[Austerity, derived from Definition~\ref{def:axiom}]\label{princ:austerity}
Under the commitment that Definition~\ref{def:axiom} ($0\neq 1$) is the
sole pre-mathematical input of the cascade series
(Section~\ref{sec:starting-points}), any construction introducing content
beyond what Definition~\ref{def:axiom} forces is inadmissible. This
excludes:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] \emph{Parameter economy.} No free parameter is introduced ---
no unexplained numerical, categorical, or symmetry-group input.
\item[(ii)] \emph{Minimal strength.} No assumption strictly stronger than
Definition~\ref{def:axiom} is invoked where a weaker sufficient assumption
exists. Strength is measured in the sense of reverse mathematics over
the base theory $\mathrm{RCA}_0$
\cite{friedman1975, simpson2009}: an assumption $A$ is strictly
stronger than $B$ if $\mathrm{RCA}_0 + A \vdash B$ but
$\mathrm{RCA}_0 + B \nvdash A$.
\item[(iii)] \emph{Unobservable quotient.} Any continuous symmetry whose
action is unobservable from within the structure is quotiented out.
\end{itemize}
\end{principle}

\begin{proof}[Derivation from Definition~\ref{def:axiom}]
Each clause identifies a distinct way a construction can violate the
sole-input commitment of Definition~\ref{def:axiom}.

\emph{Clause (i).} A free parameter is a second explicit input alongside
Definition~\ref{def:axiom}. Admitting it contradicts the sole-input
status directly.

\emph{Clause (ii).} If a construction uses assumption $A$ where the
weaker $B$ suffices (with $A\Rightarrow B$ strictly), the residue
$A\setminus B$ is additional content not forced by
Definition~\ref{def:axiom}. That residue is a second input --- the same
class of violation as clause~(i).

\emph{Clause (iii).} A continuous symmetry whose action is unobservable
from within the structure carries structural content with no derivation
consequence. Retaining it imports content not forced by
Definition~\ref{def:axiom} --- the same class of violation.

\emph{Exhaustiveness of (i)--(iii) (formal-extension trichotomy).} A
construction $C$ violates sole-input iff $C$ is derivable in some
formal system $\Sigma_C$ properly extending $\Sigma_0 = (\text{logic})
+ \{0\neq 1\}$. Standard proof theory characterises a formal system by
three independent axes --- its non-logical axioms, its underlying
logic, and its language/signature. Any extension of $\Sigma_0$ acts on
at least one of these axes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Extending the axioms imports content as a fresh assumption
(numerical, categorical, or symmetry-group input). This is clause~(i).
\item Extending the logic imports content as inferential strength
beyond what Definition~\ref{def:axiom} requires (e.g.\ adopting
classical reasoning, the axiom of choice, or higher-order quantification
where the cascade's chain demands only weaker resources); the residue
$A\setminus B$ between the adopted logic $A$ and the strictly weaker
sufficient $B$ encodes the imported content. This is clause~(ii).
\item Extending the signature or background imports content as ambient
structure with no cascade-internal observable trace --- a continuous
symmetry whose action is invisible from within the cascade. This is
clause~(iii).
\end{itemize}
These three axes exhaust the formal anatomy of an extending system:
there is no fourth axis along which a formal system can be extended in
standard proof theory. Any candidate fourth clause therefore either
reduces to one of (i)--(iii) under the standard decomposition or fails
to identify content actually present in $\Sigma_C$'s extension of
$\Sigma_0$. Definitional extensions of $\Sigma_0$ are conservative
(they introduce notation but no new theorems) and therefore do not
qualify as content additions; they require no clause.

All three clauses track a single condition: admitting content not forced
by the sole input. Austerity is therefore the operational content of
Definition~\ref{def:axiom}'s status as sole pre-mathematical input, not
a separate axiom.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textbf{Status.} Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} is a
\emph{derivation rule} for cascading from $0\neq 1$, analogous to
modus ponens being the inference rule of propositional logic: it is
built into what ``sole pre-mathematical input'' means, rather than a
further axiom within the mathematical content. Each step of the prelude
chain that previously relied on an implicit parsimony choice is now
annotated with the clause of Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} it invokes.
Whether austerity \emph{uniquely} determines $B^\infty$ --- as opposed to
rendering it defensibly minimal among a larger austerity-compatible set ---
remains open (Section~\ref{sec:open-questions}).

The question is: what does $0\neq 1$ \emph{imply}?

\section{Distinction Implies Orthogonality}
% ============================================================

If $0$ and $1$ are distinguishable, they are maximally
distinguishable. There is no sense in which $0$ partially overlaps
with $1$; they share no content. In the language of geometry, they are
\emph{orthogonal}: their inner product is zero.

This is not a metaphor. The logical operation NOT, which maps
$0\mapsto 1$ and $1\mapsto 0$, is an involution ($\mathrm{NOT}^2 =
\mathrm{Id}$) that exchanges two states sharing no common component.
In any inner-product space, the unique pair of unit vectors with no
shared component is the orthogonal pair. The passage from
$0\neq 1$ to orthogonality is not an additional assumption; it is a
translation of the same fact into geometric language.

\begin{theorem}[Distinction is orthogonality]\label{thm:orth}
Let $V$ be an inner-product space and let $e_0, e_1\in V$ be unit
vectors representing two fully distinguishable states---states sharing
no common component under any linear operation on $V$. Then
$\langle e_0, e_1\rangle = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
In an inner-product space, $\langle e_0, e_1\rangle$ is the component of
$e_1$ along $e_0$: the projection of $e_1$ onto the line through $e_0$ is
$P_{e_0}(e_1) = \langle e_0, e_1\rangle\,e_0$. ``Fully distinguishable''
states share no common component by definition, so for every projector
this projection must vanish. Applied to $P_{e_0}$: if
$\langle e_0,e_1\rangle\neq 0$, then a linear operation on $e_1$ (projection
onto $e_0$) produces a nonzero multiple of $e_0$---a partial identification
of $e_1$ with $e_0$, contradicting full distinguishability. Hence
$\langle e_0, e_1\rangle = 0$. The argument uses only the geometric
interpretation of the inner product: no probability measure, no Born rule,
and no fidelity relation is invoked.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Status of Theorem~\ref{thm:orth}]
Theorem~\ref{thm:orth} unpacks the inner-product realisation of
distinction: given the premise that distinguishable states share no
common component in an inner-product space, the projector calculation
yields orthogonality. The substantive structural content is therefore
in the premise rather than in the conclusion. The bridge from logical
$0\neq 1$ (Definition~\ref{def:axiom}) to that premise---i.e., the
choice of inner-product geometry as the realisation of logical
distinction---is a separate structural commitment, flagged as open in
Section~\ref{sec:open-questions}, item~(3). Theorem~\ref{thm:orth} is
better read as the geometric expression of that commitment than as an
independent derivation of orthogonality.
\end{remark}

\section{Orthogonality Iterates}
% ============================================================

One distinction gives two orthogonal directions and a
one-dimensional space. But the existence of one distinction does not
preclude a second. If $e_0$ and $e_1$ span a plane, a third
direction $e_2$ can be independent of both. There is no logical
obstruction: the fact that $0\neq 1$ does not limit the number of
independent things that can be distinguished.

\begin{theorem}[Countable independence]\label{thm:iterate}
Let $\{e_0, e_1\}$ be an orthogonal pair. For any finite $n$, there
exists a unit vector $e_n$ orthogonal to $\{e_0,\ldots,e_{n-1}\}$.
The iteration extends to countable infinity: the resulting structure
is a separable infinite-dimensional inner-product space (a Hilbert
space).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Existence of arbitrarily many orthogonal directions.} At each step,
Gram--Schmidt orthogonalisation produces a unit vector in the orthogonal
complement of the span of the previous vectors, as long as the ambient
space has dimension greater than the number already constructed. The
construction is recursive arithmetic on the index $n$ and is provable in
$\mathrm{RCA}_0$ \cite{simpson2009} --- the strength-measurement base
of Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} clause~(ii)
(Section~\ref{sec:open-questions}, item~(2)). So for any finite $n$,
an $(n{+}1)$-th orthogonal unit vector can be constructed.

\emph{Dimension is either $0$ or $\infty$ (clause~(i) of
Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}).} It remains to show that the iteration
must terminate at countable infinity rather than at any finite $n$.
Suppose the iteration stops at some finite $n<\infty$. Then $n$ itself
is a structural input of the theory --- an unexplained integer
specifying where distinction ceases. By Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}
clause~(i) (parameter economy), any such integer is rejected as a free
parameter. The only stopping points compatible with clause~(i) are
$n=0$ (which contradicts Definition~\ref{def:axiom}, requiring at least
two distinct states) and $n=\infty$. The iteration therefore extends
to countable infinity. (``$n=\infty$'' here is not itself a chosen
cardinality among alternatives: within the $\mathrm{RCA}_0$
strength-measurement base, $\aleph_0$ is the unique unbounded notion
--- $\mathrm{RCA}_0$ has no machinery to express higher cardinals ---
so $n=\infty$ is the unbounded countable limit the iteration's
non-termination directly produces, not a selection from a menu of
infinities.)

\emph{Countable rather than uncountable (clause~(ii) of
Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}).} The iterated Gram--Schmidt
construction generates a countable orthonormal family
$\{e_i\}_{i\in\mathbb{N}}$ provable in $\mathrm{RCA}_0$
\cite{simpson2009}. Uncountable orthonormal distinction would require
a family indexed by a higher cardinal --- a notion not expressible
within second-order arithmetic at all. Admitting it requires a
strictly stronger ambient framework (ZFC or comparable) in the sense
of clause~(ii) (Section~\ref{sec:open-questions}, item~(2); minimal
strength), and Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} clause~(ii) therefore
excludes the uncountable case. (Informally: each distinction is a
single bit --- a binary test ``is the state $e_i$ or not?'' resolvable
in finitely many steps; countably many such tests generate the
orthonormal family, while uncountably many would require distinctions
not resolvable by any countable sequence of binary tests.) The Cauchy
completion of the pre-Hilbert space generated by
$\{e_i\}_{i\in\mathbb{N}}$ is a separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert
space $\mathcal{H}$, calibrating at $\mathrm{ACA}_0$ within the Big
Five hierarchy. Whether this specific completion is uniquely forced
by Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}, or merely the most economical
choice among compatible functional-analytic refinements (rigged
Hilbert spaces, nuclear spaces, weak-topology variants), is flagged
as open in Section~\ref{sec:open-questions}, item~(1).
\end{proof}

\section{Scale Invariance and the Projective Starting Object}
% ============================================================

Starting from nothing, there is no external reference against which to
measure absolute magnitude. There is no metre stick, no Planck mass, no
energy scale. The only quantities that can be defined without an external
reference are \emph{ratios}. Equivalently: the dilation $x\mapsto\lambda x$
for $\lambda\in\mathbb{R}^{*}$ is unobservable from within the structure.
By Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} clause~(iii) (unobservable quotient),
the starting object is taken invariant under this dilation. The cascade
accordingly works with the projective quotient, not with representatives
carrying a distinguished length scale.

\noindent\textbf{The austerity-compatible quotients at this stage:
$\mathbb{R}^*$ and $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$.} Clause~(iii) of
Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} applies to continuous symmetries
\emph{defined on the current structure} whose action is unobservable
from within it. At the Prelude stage, $\mathcal{H}$ is a real Hilbert
space; the candidate symmetries split into those quotiented and those
not, for specific reasons:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Full conformal group} $\mathbb{R}^* \times \mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$.
The orthogonal-rotation factor $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ acts on the
labeling of the orthonormal frame, not on the frame's existence. The
frame itself --- its orthonormality, its countable dimension, its
inner-product geometry --- is observable structural content forced by
Theorem~\ref{thm:iterate}. The labeling within the frame is gauge: every
cascade calculation downstream (the slicing recurrence of
Section~\ref{sec:nothing-unstable}, the four distinguished dimensions of
Part~0, the cascade invariant) is $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$-invariant,
operating on the dimension parameter $d$ and on rotation-invariant
intrinsic geometric quantities (volumes, sphere areas, Gamma function
values). $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ is therefore unobservable from
within the cascade, and clause~(iii) quotients it. Both
$\mathbb{R}^*$-dilation and $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ are
austerity-compatible quotients at this stage; together they exhaust
the unobservable continuous symmetries of $\mathcal{H}$, and the cascade
takes both quotients (see ``The collapsed substrate'' paragraph below).
\item \emph{$\mathbb{C}^*$-phase rotation}. A phase action requires a
complex structure $J$ with $J^2=-\mathrm{Id}$. No such structure is
present at the Prelude stage: $\mathcal{H}$ is constructed over
$\mathbb{R}$ (see Section~\ref{sec:what-not}, ``Real start, complex
amplitudes, quaternionic spacetime''). The forced precession $\alpha=\pi/2$
of Paper~II induces $J$ downstream, at which point whether $\mathbb{C}^*$
is observable or unobservable becomes a separate question for Paper~II
and beyond. At the Prelude stage there is no $\mathbb{C}^*$ action to
quotient.
\item \emph{Discrete scale invariance} (quotient by a specific ratio
$\lambda = r$ for some fixed $r$). Selecting a specific $r$ introduces
an unexplained numerical input, violating clause~(i) (parameter
economy). A continuous quotient has no such free parameter; only
discrete reductions introduce one.
\end{itemize}
Among the continuous symmetries defined on the real Hilbert space at
the Prelude stage, two are austerity-compatible quotients ---
$\mathbb{R}^*$-dilation (radial; identifies points along each ray) and
$\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ (transverse; identifies different rays).
Both are unobservable from within the cascade, both are continuous, and
both are parameter-free. Clause~(iii) quotients both. The remaining
candidate symmetries ($\mathbb{C}^*$-phase, discrete scale invariance)
fail for the reasons above and are not quotiented at this stage.

\noindent\textbf{The collapsed substrate.} The combined quotient
$\mathcal{H}/(\mathbb{R}^*\times\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H}))$ collapses to
a single point: $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ acts transitively on rays in
the separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, so all rays are
identified, and at every finite $d$ the analogous quotient
$\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^{d-1}/\mathrm{SO}(d) = \{*\}$. This collapse is
not a vacuous result --- it is the cascade's starting condition.
Structured nothing has full rotational symmetry: at $d=\infty$ no
direction is privileged, no basis is canonical, and the only data the
substrate carries is the bare fact of being an infinite-dimensional
unit ball. The dimension parameter $d$ survives both quotients (it is
counting data, not a direction), and at each $d$ the intrinsic
geometric content of the unit ball $B^d$ --- its volume $V_d$, its
boundary sphere area $\Omega_{d-1}$, the slicing-recurrence ratio
$R(d)$, the decay rate $p(d)$, the effective slicing radius
$R_{\mathrm{eff}}(d)$ --- is rotation-invariant and non-trivial. The
cascade is the trajectory of this intrinsic content, parameterised by
$d$, unfolded from the collapsed substrate via the slicing recurrence
(Section~\ref{sec:nothing-unstable}). The cascade IS the process of
collapse to a point at each dimension; what varies between dimensions
is the rotation-invariant intrinsic geometry, and that geometry is
what every downstream theorem of Part~0 onwards computes.

\noindent\textbf{Computational representative.} Although the substrate
is the collapsed quotient, the cascade computes in the
$\mathbb{R}^*$-quotient
\[
\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H} \;=\; (\mathcal{H}\setminus\{0\})/\!\sim,\qquad
x\sim\lambda x\ \text{for}\ \lambda\in\mathbb{R}^{*}.
\]
This is the largest quotient that retains the inner-product structure
needed for the slicing recurrence and the Gram-Schmidt machinery.
Cascade calculations performed on $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$ are
$\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$-equivariant by construction --- they
descend to the collapsed quotient $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}/\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$
because their content (volumes, areas, dimension counts, Gamma function
identities) is invariant under any choice of frame. Unit norm is not
an additional axiom imposed on $\mathcal{H}$; it is the choice of a
canonical \emph{representative} of each equivalence class in
$\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$, and the choice of frame within $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$
is a further gauge fixing that washes out under $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$
when the calculation completes.

\begin{definition}[Sphere representative and ball completion]\label{def:ball}
The unit sphere $S^{d-1}=\partial B^d$ is the canonical 2-fold
representative of $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$: each ray through the origin
meets $S^{d-1}$ in exactly one antipodal pair, so $S^{d-1}$ covers
$\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$ 2-to-1. The unit ball
$B^d = \{x\in\mathbb{R}^d : |x|\leq 1\}$ is the convex completion of
this representative: each ray meets $B^d\setminus\{0\}$ in a half-open
radial segment rather than at a single point, so the ball is not
itself a section of $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$ but contains $S^{d-1}$ as
its boundary. The ball organises the slicing recurrence
(Remark below); the sphere is where the cascade's content lives. Every
subsequent theorem of the cascade is projective-invariant---it depends
only on the ratio structure of $\mathcal{H}$, not on the chosen
representative or its completion---so the joint choice is
computational, not ontological.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why the ball rather than the sphere]
We work with $B^\infty$ rather than $S^\infty$ because the ball contains the
entire tower $\{S^{d-1}\}_{d\geq 1}$ as cross-sections of a single operation:
slicing $B^{d+1}$ perpendicular to any axis at height $x\in[-1,1]$ yields a
ball $B^d$ of radius $\sqrt{1-x^2}$, whose boundary is a sphere $S^{d-1}$.
This is the slicing recurrence of Section~7, and it is the mechanism by
which the Gamma function enters. The sphere $S^d$ does not carry an
analogous native recurrence to lower-dimensional spheres; the ball is the
minimal container in which the recurrence is expressible without auxiliary
structure. Once the recurrence is in place, boundary dominance
($\Omega_{d-1}/V_d = d$) shows that the spheres carry essentially all the
content---so physics lives on the sphere, but the recurrence is organised
by the ball.
\end{remark}

From Section~4: the natural $\mathcal{H}$ is an infinite-dimensional
separable real Hilbert space. From scale invariance: the starting object is
$\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$. Working in $B^\infty$, the convex completion of
the canonical sphere representative (Definition~\ref{def:ball}):

\begin{corollary}[The starting point]\label{cor:start}
The mathematical object corresponding to ``nothing with the capacity for
distinction'' is $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$, represented for calculation by
$B^\infty$, the infinite-dimensional unit ball.
\end{corollary}

\section{Nothing Is Empty}
% ============================================================

The infinite-dimensional unit ball is a legitimate mathematical
object---but it is empty in every measurable sense.

\begin{theorem}[Geometric nothing]\label{thm:nothing}
$B^\infty$ has zero volume, zero surface area, and no interior
point is farther than $\varepsilon$ from the boundary for any
$\varepsilon > 0$ (concentration of measure).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$V_d = \pi^{d/2}/\Gamma(d/2+1)\to 0$ as $d\to\infty$.
$\Omega_{d-1} = 2\pi^{d/2}/\Gamma(d/2)\to 0$ as $d\to\infty$. By
concentration of measure on $S^{d-1}$, the uniform distribution on
$B^d$ places a fraction $1 - (1-\varepsilon^2/d)^{d/2} \to
1 - e^{-\varepsilon^2/2}$ of its mass within a shell of thickness
$\varepsilon/\sqrt{d}$ of the boundary.
\end{proof}

$B^\infty$ has no volume to fill, no boundary to inhabit, no
interior to explore. It is the geometric expression of ``distinction
exists but content does not.'' It is structured nothing.

\section{Nothing Is Unstable}\label{sec:nothing-unstable}
% ============================================================

$B^\infty$ is empty---but it is not inert. It has a slicing
recurrence: cut $B^{d+1}$ perpendicular to one axis, and each
cross-section is a $d$-ball of radius $\sqrt{1-x^2}$. The volumes
obey:
\[
V_{d+1} = V_d \cdot \sqrt{\pi}\cdot R(d+1), \qquad R(d) =
\frac{\Gamma((d+1)/2)}{\Gamma((d+2)/2)}.
\]
The constant $\sqrt{\pi}$ is forced by orthogonality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:orth}): the angle between axis and equator is
$\pi/2$, forcing the half-integer argument in $B(1/2,\cdot)$, giving
$\Gamma(1/2) = \sqrt{\pi}$.

As $d$ decreases from infinity, volumes \emph{grow}. The ball acquires
content it did not have. The volume peaks at $d = 5$. Nothing, viewed
from a lower dimension, is no longer nothing.

This is the instability: $B^\infty$ has zero volume, but its
finite-dimensional cross-sections do not. The descent from $d=\infty$
to finite $d$ is a transition from emptiness to content, governed
entirely by the Gamma function, with no free parameters.

\begin{remark}
The word ``descent'' does not imply a process in time. It is a
mathematical relationship: the volumes and areas of unit balls at
successive finite dimensions are related by the slicing recurrence.
Time will emerge later (Part~II) as the cascade's own orthogonal
slicing direction. The descent is the static structure from which
time is read off.
\end{remark}

\section{The Destination Is Forced}
% ============================================================

The volume maximum is at $d = 5$: the unique dimension where the unit
ball has the largest volume. The observer sits at $d = 4$, the
boundary $S^3$ of $B^5$. By boundary dominance ($\Omega_{d-1}/V_d =
d$), this boundary carries $4/5$ of the content.

Why does the observer end up here? Part~III of the series proves
that $d = 4$ is the unique spacetime dimension satisfying two
independent constraints simultaneously:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Lovelock uniqueness}: the gravitational equation has
a unique form (Einstein's equation with cosmological constant) only
at $d = 4$.
\item \textbf{Complex spinors}: the Clifford algebra
$\mathrm{Cl}(1,d-1)$ has irreducibly complex minimal spinors---the
prerequisite for quantum mechanics---only when $d\bmod 8\in\{4,5,6\}$.
\end{enumerate}

The intersection is $\{4\}$. No other dimension permits both
unique physical laws and quantum information processing. The observer
is not placed at $d = 4$ by assumption; $d = 4$ is where the
mathematics forces the unique coincidence of stable laws, complex
phase, and maximal content.

\section{The Chain}
% ============================================================

The complete logical chain, from pre-mathematics to physics, is:

\section{What This Paper Does Not Do}
\label{sec:what-not}
% ============================================================

\textbf{Does not derive physics.} This prelude contains no physical
predictions. Its purpose is to argue that the starting point of the
series --- the infinite-dimensional unit ball --- is \emph{defensibly
minimal} given the single declared pre-mathematical input
(Definition~\ref{def:axiom}, $0\neq 1$) and the austerity derivation
(Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}) that follows from its sole-input
status. The chain makes explicit which austerity clause is invoked at
each step: clauses~(i) and~(ii) at step~2 (parameter economy forcing
infinite dimension; minimal strength forcing countable over uncountable
dimension via binary-test resolvability); clause~(iii) at step~3
(unobservable quotient, applied to both $\mathbb{R}^*$-dilation and
$\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$, collapsing $\mathcal{H}$ to a single
substrate point at $d=\infty$ from which intrinsic content unfolds via
the slicing recurrence). Whether the combined input
(Definition~\ref{def:axiom} plus austerity) forces $B^\infty$
\emph{uniquely}, or merely renders it the most austere defensible
starting object, is the exploratory question the prelude asks. The
cascade series' load-bearing hypothesis (cover sheet) is independent
of whichever answer is reached here: the downstream derivations take
$B^\infty$ as given and test it by consequence.

\textbf{Real start, complex amplitudes, quaternionic spacetime.} The
Prelude's starting object is over $\mathbb{R}$: no complex or quaternionic
structure is introduced by hand. By Hurwitz's theorem, the associative
normed division algebras over $\mathbb{R}$ are exactly $\mathbb{R}$,
$\mathbb{C}$, and $\mathbb{H}$, and the Gamma function governs the slicing
recurrence in all three cases. The cascade selects between them downstream:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Real at the start.} The real Hilbert space is the most austere
starting point and is the one used here. No complex or quaternionic
structure is assumed.
\item \emph{Complex at the amplitude level.} A complex structure
$J$ with $J^2=-\mathrm{Id}$ arises as a \emph{derived} consequence of the
forced precession $\alpha=\pi/2$ in Part~II. It is a theorem, not an axiom:
two consecutive cascade quarter-turns compose to multiplication by $-1$,
promoting the state space to a complex Hilbert space at the quantum layer.
\item \emph{Quaternionic at the spacetime dimension.} Lovelock uniqueness
intersected with Clifford periodicity forces the spacetime dimension to
$d=4=\dim_\mathbb{R}\mathbb{H}$ in Part~III. The \emph{spacetime} dimension
matches the quaternions while the \emph{amplitude} algebra remains
$\mathbb{C}$.
\end{itemize}
The real/complex/quaternionic hierarchy is thus not an open question: each
algebra enters the cascade at the layer forced by a downstream theorem.
What the Prelude does not do is prove that the cascade could not have been
written over $\mathbb{C}$ or $\mathbb{H}$ from the outset---it proves only
that the real case suffices, and that the complex and quaternionic roles
are recovered downstream without additional input.

\textbf{Does not resolve the hard problem.} The cascade derives the
dimension where self-awareness is mathematically possible. It does
not derive self-awareness itself. The question ``why is there
something rather than nothing?''\ receives an answer: because
nothing, taken seriously, has structure. The question ``why is there
\emph{experience}?''\ remains open.

\section{Open Questions}
\label{sec:open-questions}
% ============================================================

Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} derives three previously-implicit
parsimony choices (finite-vs-$\aleph_0$ dimension;
countable-vs-uncountable independence; the unobservable-continuous-symmetry
quotients of $\mathbb{R}^*$-dilation and $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$
together collapsing $\mathcal{H}$ to a single substrate point at
$d=\infty$) as clauses of a single derivation rule from
Definition~\ref{def:axiom}. The prelude chain now invokes these
clauses explicitly, and austerity is derived from the sole-input
status of $0\neq 1$ rather than declared as a second input. What
remains open:

\textbf{(1) Uniqueness of $B^\infty$ under austerity.} Is the
cascade's starting substrate (the collapsed quotient
$\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}/\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ of a separable
infinite-dimensional real Hilbert space, with $B^\infty$ as its
geometric realisation at $d=\infty$ and $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$ as the
computational representative) the \emph{unique} mathematical structure
derivable from Definition~\ref{def:axiom} under
Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}, or merely one of several
austerity-compatible starting substrates?

Two classes of alternatives are explicitly ruled out inside this
prelude:
\begin{itemize}
\item[$\bullet$] \emph{Complex or quaternionic Hilbert space as starting
point.} Ruled out by clause~(ii) (minimal strength): starting over
$\mathbb{C}$ or $\mathbb{H}$ is strictly stronger than starting over
$\mathbb{R}$, and the latter suffices at this stage. Section~\ref{sec:what-not}
establishes that the real case is the most austere starting point; $J$
with $J^2=-\mathrm{Id}$ is recovered as a Paper~II \emph{theorem} from
the forced precession, not imposed upstream.
\item[$\bullet$] \emph{Non-separable (uncountable-dimensional) Hilbert
space.} Ruled out inside Theorem~\ref{thm:iterate} by clause~(ii) via
binary-test resolvability.
\end{itemize}
Alternatives that remain open candidates:
\begin{itemize}
\item[$\bullet$] Non-Hilbert inner-product topologies compatible with
clauses~(i)--(iii): weak-topology variants, rigged Hilbert spaces,
nuclear spaces, and similar functional-analytic refinements. The
Prelude's chain does not rule these out explicitly.
\item[$\bullet$] Structures that agree with $\mathbb{P}\mathcal{H}$ on
the ``projective content'' but differ in finite-rank corrections or
pathological embeddings not relevant to any downstream cascade
calculation. These may be mathematically distinct but physically
indistinguishable.
\end{itemize}
A uniqueness theorem of the form ``the unique structure derivable from
Definition~\ref{def:axiom} under Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}, up to
isomorphism, is $B^\infty$'' would close this question and promote the
prelude from \emph{defensibly minimal} to \emph{forced}. In its absence,
the prelude's conclusion is conditional on the §5 enumeration plus the
clauses of Section~\ref{sec:what-not} exhausting relevant candidates.

\textbf{(2) Formal measure of assumption strength (clause (ii)) ---
resolved.} The partial order on assumption strength used in clause~(ii)
of Principle~\ref{princ:austerity} is the reverse-mathematics ordering
over the base theory $\mathrm{RCA}_0$
\cite{friedman1975, simpson2009}: $A$ is strictly stronger than $B$ if
$\mathrm{RCA}_0 + A \vdash B$ but $\mathrm{RCA}_0 + B \nvdash A$. This
framework calibrates the strength of mathematical theorems against the
``Big Five'' subsystems of second-order arithmetic
($\mathrm{RCA}_0 \subsetneq \mathrm{WKL}_0 \subsetneq \mathrm{ACA}_0
\subsetneq \mathrm{ATR}_0 \subsetneq \Pi^1_1\text{-}\mathrm{CA}_0$),
giving a precise quantitative measure of any cascade derivation's
foundational footprint. The countable-vs-uncountable comparison
invoked in Theorem~\ref{thm:iterate} is a clean reverse-mathematics
statement: the iterated Gram--Schmidt construction generating a
countable orthonormal family is provable in $\mathrm{RCA}_0$;
uncountable orthonormal distinction in the prelude's sense requires
expressive machinery beyond second-order arithmetic. The cascade's
downstream content --- classical analysis, the Gamma function and
its analytic properties, Hilbert-space separability with Cauchy
completion --- calibrates at or below $\mathrm{ACA}_0$ within the Big
Five hierarchy, so the cascade's foundational footprint is bounded by
a well-understood, modest fragment of second-order arithmetic. The
choice of $\mathrm{RCA}_0$ as the strength-measurement base is a
foundational commitment, not a cascade-internal axiom: it specifies
how clause~(ii) is to be applied without altering what the cascade
proves from $0\neq 1$.

\textbf{(3) The interpretive bridge at Theorem~\ref{thm:orth} ---
essentially closed via Sol\`er.} The step from logical $0\neq 1$ to
geometric ``states sharing no common component in an inner-product
space'' (the premise of Theorem~\ref{thm:orth}) is a structural
interpretation, not a clause of austerity. Austerity governs
\emph{which} structure is admissible once the interpretive framework
is fixed; it does not fix the interpretive framework itself. Three
sub-questions:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] Is the inner-product-space interpretation of $0\neq 1$ the
unique minimal geometric realisation, or are there weaker structures
(e.g.\ topological distinction without an inner product) that also admit
the Prelude's subsequent chain?
\item[(b)] Does the sole-input commitment of
Definition~\ref{def:axiom} --- from which Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}
is derived --- extend to the choice of interpretive framework?
Specifically: can one show that ``inner-product space as geometric
realisation of $0\neq 1$'' is the minimal interpretation in a sense
analogous to clauses~(i)--(iii) of Principle~\ref{princ:austerity}, or
does choosing an interpretive framework require a further
meta-principle beyond sole-input status?
\item[(c)] Alternatively, is the inner-product interpretation itself a
theorem of some more austere framework (for instance, as the unique
realisation admitting the NOT involution and its projection $P_{e_0}$
simultaneously)?
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Resolution of (a) and (c) via Sol\`er's convergence
theorem.} Sub-questions (a) and (c) admit a strong positive answer
through the convergence theorem of Sol\`er
\cite{soler1995}: any irreducible orthomodular lattice satisfying
\begin{itemize}
\item[$\bullet$] the existence of an infinite orthogonal sequence,
\item[$\bullet$] transitive action of the automorphism group on
atoms, and
\item[$\bullet$] a regularity condition on the modular structure (the
existence of a sequence of orthogonal atoms whose pairwise
``Pythagorean weights'' are equal)
\end{itemize}
is isomorphic to the lattice of closed subspaces of a separable
Hilbert space over $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{C}$, or $\mathbb{H}$.

The cascade's chain naturally satisfies these hypotheses: the
iteration of Theorem~\ref{thm:iterate} produces the infinite orthogonal
sequence; the $\mathrm{SO}(\mathcal{H})$ action recognised as gauge of
the labeling in Section~5 provides the transitive automorphism on
atoms; and the projector apparatus underlying Theorem~\ref{thm:orth}'s
premise (``states share no common component under any linear
operation'') gives the orthomodular lattice structure
($A\wedge B^\perp = 0$ in lattice language). Combined with Hurwitz's
theorem (Section~\ref{sec:what-not}, ``Real start, complex amplitudes,
quaternionic spacetime''), which restricts the associative normed
division algebras over $\mathbb{R}$ to exactly $\mathbb{R}$,
$\mathbb{C}$, $\mathbb{H}$, the geometric realisation of $0\neq 1$
admitting the Prelude's chain is therefore pinned down to Hilbert
space over a Hurwitz division algebra. Clause~(ii) (minimal strength)
then selects $\mathbb{R}$.

This essentially closes sub-questions (a) and (c): the inner-product
interpretation is forced, up to choice of $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{C}$,
$\mathbb{H}$, by the cascade's own structural requirements, and is a
theorem of orthomodular-lattice theory rather than a free choice. The
``more austere framework'' of (c) is exactly orthomodular lattices: the
NOT involution (orthocomplement) and the projection $P_{e_0}$ jointly
generate the lattice structure, and Sol\`er's theorem then forces the
inner-product realisation. Sub-question (b) --- whether sole-input
commitment formally extends to the choice of orthomodular framework
as the minimal interpretation --- remains open as a meta-foundational
question parallel to the choice of standard mathematics as the
ambient framework.

\noindent\textbf{Foundational cost.} Sol\`er's theorem uses classical
proof machinery (Zorn's lemma and full choice in the original proof)
and calibrates above $\mathrm{ACA}_0$ in the Big Five hierarchy of
item~(2), likely requiring ZFC-level machinery. Adopting Sol\`er to
close (a) and (c) therefore relaxes the cascade's foundational
footprint from ``at or below $\mathrm{ACA}_0$'' to ``$\mathrm{ACA}_0$
for the cascade's own derivations, plus ZFC for the interpretive
bridge's closure.'' The cascade's specific predictions remain at
$\mathrm{ACA}_0$; only the proof that no alternative geometric
realisation exists requires the higher strength. This is a one-way
asymmetric cost: the cascade can be \emph{used} at $\mathrm{ACA}_0$
strength while the meta-claim ``Hilbert space is forced'' lives at
ZFC strength.

\textbf{(4) The hard problem.} The cascade derives the
dimension where self-awareness is mathematically possible. It does not
derive self-awareness itself (already noted in Section~\ref{sec:what-not}).
