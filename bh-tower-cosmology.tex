\[

\]
(expected analytic eigenvalues \(E_n = 2n + 1\) for \(n = 0,1,2,\dots\)) on a uniform grid using a second-order central-difference stencil.

We employ \(N = 1201\) points over the domain \([-15,15]\). The sparse-matrix Hamiltonian is constructed and the lowest ten eigenvalues are extracted with a shifted-inverse eigensolver. The Python implementation is:

\begin{verbatim}
import numpy as np
from scipy.sparse import diags
from scipy.sparse.linalg import eigs

N = 1201
L = 15.0
x = np.linspace(-L, L, N)
dx = x[1] - x[0]

# Negative Laplacian
off_diag = -1.0 / dx**2 * np.ones(N-1)
diag    =  2.0 / dx**2 * np.ones(N)
lap_neg = diags([off_diag, diag, off_diag], [-1, 0, 1], format='csr')

V = diags([x**2], [0], format='csr')
H = lap_neg + V

vals = eigs(H, k=10, which='SM', return_eigenvectors=False)
evals = np.sort(np.real(vals))

\section{Appendix B: sech² Soliton Derivation}
\label{app:sech2}

We work in the effective five-dimensional description obtained from the radial projection of the plenum onto the lowest ($k=1$) marginally stable interface. The line element is taken in the standard warped form
\[
ds^2 = e^{2A(y)}\eta_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu dx^\nu + dy^2,

The Israel junction condition for a thin positive-tension brane (surface energy density $\sigma>0$) located at $y=0$ with $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry implies a discontinuity in the first derivative of the warp factor:
\[
A'(0^+) - A'(0^-) = -\frac{4\pi G_5}{3}\sigma,
\]
where $G_5$ is the five-dimensional Newton constant (normalised such that the overall Planck scale will be fixed later by the regulator core). In the thin-brane limit this produces the familiar RS kink $A(y)=-k|y|$, but the regulator core of the zeta-regularized vacuum energy smooths the source into a finite-width distribution.

Differentiating the integrated Israel condition (or, equivalently, extracting the $yy$-component of the five-dimensional Einstein equations with the effective source $\rho(y)$ that includes the zeta-regularized contribution $\lambda=\zeta(-1)=-1/12$) yields the second-order ordinary differential equation
\[
A''(y) = -\frac{4\pi G_5}{3}\rho(y).
\]
The unique soliton profile compatible with marginal stability and the observed Planck scale is the sech² distribution
\[
\rho(y) = \rho_0\,\sech^2\left(\frac{y}{r_c}\right),
\]
where $\rho_0$ is the peak energy density of the regulator core. Substituting into the ODE and integrating twice with the boundary conditions $A'(\pm\infty)=0$ (asymptotic flatness) and $A'(0)=0$ (symmetry about the core) gives
\[
A'(y) = -\frac{C}{r_c}\tanh\left(\frac{y}{r_c}\right), \qquad
A(y) = -C\ln\left(\cosh\frac{y}{r_c}\right)+D.
\]
The overall scale can be absorbed into the definition of $r_c$ by setting the dimensionless constant $C=1$ (standard normalisation in thick-brane literature). This yields the exact analytic solution
\[
A(y) = -\ln\left(\cosh\frac{y}{r_c}\right).
\]

Direct differentiation confirms consistency:
\begin{align*}
A'(y) &= -\frac{1}{r_c}\tanh\left(\frac{y}{r_c}\right), \\
A''(y) &= -\frac{1}{r_c^2}\sech^2\left(\frac{y}{r_c}\right).
\end{align*}
Thus $A''(y)\propto-\sech^2(y/r_c)$, exactly as required by the Israel-derived ODE with the regulator source $\rho(y)$.

The curvature radius $r_c$ is fixed by matching the core curvature $A''(0)=-1/r_c^2$ to the zeta-regularized vacuum energy density $|\lambda|=1/12$ (in units where $4\pi G_5\rho_0=1$, the natural Planckian normalisation arising from the heat-kernel trace). This immediately produces
\[
r_c = \sqrt{12}.
\]

For the full tower, the condensation time $t_k=k$ of the $k$-th shell stretches the effective regulator width, yielding the family of transient black-hole branes with tension profiles
\[
\rho_k(y)\propto\sech^2\left(\frac{y}{k r_c}\right).
\]
Each shell sits at effective dimension $D_k=4+1/k$, with only the $k\to\infty$ limit remaining marginally stable. This completes the derivation of the warp factor and tension tower directly from the Israel junction condition plus the zeta-regularized regulator core.
